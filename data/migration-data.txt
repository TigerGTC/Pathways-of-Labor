# Pathways of Labor — Data

International labor migration shapes the global economy, with an estimated 167.7 million migrant workers worldwide (Migration Data Portal, accessed 2026). These corridors trace the dominant flows of labor across continents — from South and Southeast Asia to the Gulf, from Eastern Europe to Western Europe, and within Latin America and Africa. Volumes represent annual worker flow or mid-decade stock estimates drawn from ILO, UN DESA, World Bank, and official national sources.

## Migrant Worker
> A migrant worker is an individual who relocates, often temporarily, from their permanent residence to find employment, which can occur within their own country or across international borders.

Source: EBSCO Research Starters — Migrant Worker

## Outflow Country
> An outflow country (or country of origin) is a nation from which workers emigrate to seek employment abroad, typically driven by limited domestic opportunities, lower wages, or economic instability.

## Destination Country
> A destination country (or country of destination) is a nation that receives migrant workers, typically offering higher wages, labor demand, or better economic conditions that attract foreign labor.

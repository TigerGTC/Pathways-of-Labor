# Global Migrant Worker Research Data

---

## I. Defining the Subject

### Key Term: Migrant Worker
According to the International Labour Organization (ILO), a migrant worker is:
> "a person who migrates or has migrated from one country to another with a view to being employed otherwise than on his own account."

This includes contract laborers, highly skilled professionals, and seasonal workers.

### The "How" of Migration

**Legal Frameworks:** Most workers move via specific visa classes — e.g., the U.S. H-2A/H-2B programs or the GCC "Kafala" sponsorship system.

**Recruitment:** Private recruitment agencies often act as intermediaries, matching labor supply in the "Global South" with vacancies in the "Global North."

**Economic Drivers:** The primary driver is the **Wage Gap** — workers move where GDP per capita is significantly higher, to maximize the value of remittances (money sent back home).
